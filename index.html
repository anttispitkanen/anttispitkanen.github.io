Wellbeing

One of the things I value the most is both physical and mental wellbeing.

Motivation and purpose

The most crucial thing for one's wellbeing is doing things that matter to you. People have the capacity to spend enormous amounts of effort on doing things they care about and find interesting. So why do people work jobs they don't want to, in working environments where they are treated badly and not motivated?

The whole society would be so much more efficient if people were able to use their effort doing things they feel motivated about. That doesn't mean however that everyone should quit their jobs and start doing something completely different.

It means that companies should do everything in their power to make their employees feel appreciated and supported in what they do. It also means that every employee should spend some time thinking about their own wants and needs.

When applying for a job it's crucial to apply to a job you want to work on, to a company you want to work in and to a greater cause that you want to work towards.

I am a big advocate of positive psychology, happiness and mindfullness.

Exercise

Wellbeing is obviously also about the physical aspect. Taking care of just the mind is not enough.

I'm very interested in the way the human musculoskeletal system works. I have studied many aspects of physiotherapy and functional movement and try to continuously apply that knowledge to myself.

The goal is to keep the body functional and healthy. The solution is to both strengthen the muscles and make the joints and other ligaments more mobile. The tool is to exercise in diverse ways with knowledge on what to do and how.

I mostly enjoy yoga-like mobility work and bodyweight based workouts. Lately I have tried some street workout and found it very engaging.

Besides taking care of myself I try to spread the knowledge and help people feel physically better by advising them on correct working ergonomy, posture and movement mechanics.

Exploring nature

Being in the middle of nature has many benefits in and of itself. Think of a forest or a lakeside: the tranquility, fresh air and calming view. While just being in nature is good for you, the benefits really come to full effect when you add moving to the equation.

Running on a trail, jumping on rocks or climbing trees requires you to really concentrate on what you are doing. The resulting movement is versatile and challenging but you get the added benefit of mindfulness as well.

Movement that requires a lot of coordination is optimal in making your mind focuse on one thing. That gives the brain a break from the everyday multitasking. It forces you to really use your senses and live in the moment. And that is a good thing.

Community

I want to both benefit from being part of the community and give back to it.


These are some of the communities I'm involved in:

TampereJS

TampereJS is a community of JavaScript and web-development oriented people in Tampere. We organize meetups once a month with companies as sponsors. The community is completely volunteer based and the events are free to attend.

I volunteered as an organizer for three reasons. First of all I want to learn as much as possible about web development and JavaScript, and what would be a more engaging way. Secondly, I want to create myself a vast network of IT people and companies, and where would that be easier. Thirdly, I saw an opportunity for being active in creating something new for the Tampere IT scene. For me it's a win-win-win.

Check TampereJS out and come attend our meetups!

TreStart

TreStart is a community under the city of Tampere where talented people meet local companies to network and to create new business.

The services of TreStart are mainly targeted on one hand to highly educated unemployed people, or talents as we call them, and on the other hand to growing companies in the Tampere region. I first found out about TreStart and attended their events when I was unemployed myself. Through the events and actions I got to complete interesting projects with local companies and grow my professional networks with loads of potential employers, business partners and various talents.

I have gotten much out of attending TreStart events and now I want to help out the community which ever way I can. I still attend all the events possible and try to share my thoughts and tips on job seeking, personal branding and networking.

Virtual Reality Finland

VR Finland is an association that supports the development of VR and AR ecosystems in Finland. The goal is to help Finland become the leading player in VR and AR technologies.

Now that VR and AR are becoming more known and popular I want to be in the frontlines.

I've joined Virtual Reality Finland fairly recently, but I have already attended several events and even helped organize one.
